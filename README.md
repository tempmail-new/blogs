# Tempmailso Knowledge Base

This repository is shared to explain the purpose of temporary email and the practical ways people use it every day.

The goal is simple: help everyone understand when a disposable inbox is useful, when it is not, and how it can keep a main inbox cleaner.

## Why Temporary Email Matters

Temporary email is often used to create a short-lived inbox for signups, testing, and one-time messages. It can reduce spam, separate casual signups from personal accounts, and make online browsing a little easier to manage.

This is not about replacing your main email. It is about giving people a safer, simpler option for low-trust situations.

## Common Use Cases

- Signing up for newsletters you only want to read once
- Testing websites, apps, or forms without using a personal address
- Claiming a one-time coupon, download, or trial
- Keeping your main inbox cleaner during research or shopping
- Separating work, side projects, and personal browsing
- Avoiding unnecessary follow-up emails from low-value services

## Security and Safety

Temporary email can help reduce exposure to spam, but it should also be used with care.

It is especially helpful when dealing with phishing attacks, where scammers try to trick people into sharing passwords, payment details, or other personal data. A disposable inbox can lower risk in some cases by keeping untrusted signups away from your primary email account.

Still, temporary email is not a replacement for good security habits. For important accounts, always use a permanent address, strong passwords, and two-factor authentication.

## What It Is Not For

- Banking or financial accounts
- Password recovery for important services
- Long-term communication
- Any account you need to keep for a long time
